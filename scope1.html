Conclusion:

From the observation, we can conclude that people mostly behave politely online, although there are some platforms with serious users.
Facebook:
Politeness appears to be the dominant behaviour on Facebook, with 98.4% of observations indicating support or polite interactions. Aggressive behaviour is relatively rare on Facebook, with only 1.6% of observations indicating provocative or aggressive interactions.
RED:
RED seems to have a higher prevalence of aggressive behaviour compared to Facebook, with 71% of observations indicating provocative or aggressive interactions. Polite or supportive behaviour is less common on RED, accounting for only 29% of observations.
Instagram:
Instagram falls somewhere in between Facebook and RED in terms of online behaviour. Politeness is more common on Instagram, with 88.6% of observations indicating support or polite interactions. Aggressive behaviour is less common on Instagram, accounting for 11.4% of observations.
It's essential to remember that these conclusions are based on a specific set of scenarios and platforms and may not reflect overall online behaviour across the internet. Different platforms attract different user demographics, which can influence online behaviour. Additionally, the nature of the scenarios observed can also impact the results.
